Stevia vs Erythritol vs Xylitol – Which Sweetener to Choose? Comparison Guide for Food Manufacturers

As a food manufacturer, choosing the right natural sweetener can be crucial for your product's success. Stevia, erythritol, and xylitol are three of the most popular sugar-free sweeteners available on the market. In this article, we'll compare them in detail to help you choose the best sweetener for your production.

Introduction: Why Sweetener Choice Matters

Today, consumers across Europe and worldwide are increasingly looking for products without sugar or with sugar reduction. As a food manufacturer, you need to choose a sweetener that not only provides the right sweetness but also complies with EU standards, is safe for health, and suitable for your product. Stevia, erythritol, and xylitol are three popular options, but each has unique properties.

1. Stevia (E960) – The Sweetest Natural Sweetener

Stevia Rebaudiana is 200-300 times sweeter than sugar with zero calories and glycemic index = 0. Ideal for diabetic-friendly and keto products. May have a slightly bitter aftertaste in larger amounts, but is heat-stable (up to 200°C). Learn more about stevia →

2. Erythritol (E968) – Calorie-Free and Tooth-Friendly

Erythritol has 70% of sugar's sweetness with zero calories and glycemic index = 0. Valued for neutral taste (no aftertaste) and no side effects (does not cause bloating). Ideal for baked goods and products requiring heat stability. Learn more about erythritol →

3. Xylitol (E967) – Tooth-Friendly and Natural

Xylitol has the same sweetness as sugar (1:1 substitute) with 40% fewer calories. Valued for anti-cavity properties – ideal for oral care products. May cause bloating in larger amounts, but is safe for diabetics (GI=13). Learn more about xylitol →

Which Sweetener to Choose? Practical Guide

Choose Stevia if:

  • You need highest sweetness with minimal ingredient quantity
  • You produce beverages, teas, or keto products
  • You need zero calories and glycemic index = 0
  • Your product doesn't require large amounts of sweetener (stevia is very sweet)

Choose Erythritol if:

  • You need neutral taste without aftertaste
  • You produce baked goods, sugar-free chocolate, or beverages
  • You need zero calories and no side effects
  • Your product requires heat stability (baking)

Choose Xylitol if:

  • You produce chewing gum, mints, or children's products
  • You need anti-cavity properties
  • You need a 1:1 sugar substitute (same sweetness)
  • Your product doesn't require zero calories (xylitol has 40% fewer calories than sugar)

Sweetener Blends – The Best Solution?

Many food manufacturers in Europe and worldwide choose to use sweetener blends to combine the benefits of different ingredients. For example, a blend of stevia and erythritol can provide stevia's intense sweetness with erythritol's neutral taste, eliminating stevia's bitter aftertaste. A blend of xylitol and erythritol can provide full sweetness with fewer calories.

EU Standards and Certification

All three sweeteners – stevia (E960), erythritol (E968), and xylitol (E967) – are compliant with European Union standards and can be safely used in food production across Europe. At SWEETSTEVIAM, we provide full documentation and compliance certificates for all our products.

Summary: Which Sweetener to Choose?

The choice between stevia, erythritol, and xylitol depends on many factors: product type, taste requirements, budget, and target audience. Stevia is best for products requiring intense sweetness with zero calories. Erythritol is ideal for products requiring neutral taste and heat stability. Xylitol is best for products where anti-cavity properties are important.
